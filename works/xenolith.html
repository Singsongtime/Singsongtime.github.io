Xenolith
Year: 2019
Performed by KUG Aufführungspraktikum-Ensemble
Location: Ligetisaal, Kunstuniversität Graz
-----
-----
Impressions: People have described this work as brutalist, cosmic, woodcut; like a ghost-story, star-wars, or a swatch-book. To me it sounds like a kind of big slow-burning haunted snow-globe-as-literalist-object.

Details: There are two voices (one is a noisy figure, the other is a clear tone). Throughout the piece the voices trade parts as the various instrument groups rotate playing them. The tension here between noise and signal is again reflected the difference between a clean, 'strict', playing technique at the beginning (and very end) and the later addition of a distortion, a turbulence, to the playing techinques (in the middle of the piece).
