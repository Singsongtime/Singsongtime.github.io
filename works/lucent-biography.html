Lucent Biography (2019)


for six recorders

Performers: Alena Kiszter, Laura Dümpelmann, Laura Hanetseder, Lina Herman, Tzu-Chi Kuo, Zuzana Gulová.
Location: Mariatrost Basilica on June 4, 2019.


With the help of the performers, I've rewritten this piece for six recorders. I wanted this version to be somehow relevant to the space, a church, and remembered the feedback Simon Steen-Anderson gave me about the previous version: the falling lines stand out. I read the falls in this piece as allegory for mortality, death, suffering, for which Maria in the space of the performance could give solace. Meanwhile, I overlooked the more obvious reference to falling in Christianity: 'to the defiling influence of language and signification'. This piece is being notated verbally, highlighting the role of language in ordering the sounds.



Lucent Biography (2018)


for two recorders

Performers: Jasmin Vorhauser, Christine Blasl.
Location: Saint James's Church, Bergen, Norway.


One of the longest english words with all unique letters is the word 'uncopyrightable'. I constructed a musical form relevant to this word and, through rehearsing closely with the musicians, relying on a large amount of open, verbal playing instructions (rather than notes), I attempted to return a sense of uncopyrightability to the musical form abstracted from the word uncopyrightable.
