tin sundrops

percussion music for four performers

Year: 2015
Performers: Anthony Ciesielka, Jessica Chambers, HM Mease, Wyatt Wakefield
Location: Rock Hall, Temple University, Philadelphia, USA


Two makeshift acoustic instruments, made from metal, duet. Stirring or shaking, their tones change, and a little water accidentally spills onto the floor. Someone lets a coin fall, strikes a bell, pours legumes from one cup to another. The fourth person types on a makeshift 'drum'. There are flowers on the table.

This work can be read as a continuation of some paradoxical ways of composition championed by John Cage in the 1950's, including composing non-intentionality (through chance operation, graphic scores); bringing everyday objects into musical space; silence. I was taking Cage at his word, by trying to 'let sounds be themselves', to remove myself from the music. I hadn't realized at the time that trying to erase intention is itself an intention.
